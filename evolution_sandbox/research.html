Evolution Sandbox
Investigations and Lab Ideas

Core Investigations

Resource Influx vs Population Size

  • Manipulate: Food/Second (low to high).
  • Hold constant: Food Energy, Base Metabolism, Reproduction Threshold, Mutation.
  • Expectation: Higher influx raises carrying capacity; watch for overshoot/oscillations at mid values.
  • Measure: Mean population over last 2-3 minutes, peak population, extinction risk.

Food Energy vs Life-History Strategy

  • Manipulate: Food Energy (low to high).
  • Expectation: Higher energy per item favors fast reproduction; low energy favors Size (storage/reach).
  • Measure: Mean genes (Speed/Size/Sense), births per minute, age-at-first-reproduction proxy (energy build-up rate).

Metabolic Cost vs Trait Feasibility

  • Manipulate: Base Metabolism.
  • Expectation: Higher cost penalizes Speed/Sense-heavy mixes; Size may increase for storage benefit.
  • Measure: Mean genes, time to stabilization, extinction probability.

Reproduction Threshold vs r/K Analogy

  • Manipulate: Reproduction Threshold.
  • Expectation: Low threshold → more, smaller-energy parents; high threshold → fewer, investment-heavy parents.
  • Measure: Births/minute, population variance, mean age, mean energy.

Mutation Rate vs Adaptation and Stability

  • Manipulate: Mutation (std).
  • Expectation: Low → slow adaptation, risk of local optima; high → faster exploration but noisy trait means.
  • Measure: Rate of change of mean genes, diversity (spread of genes), extinction risk under changing resources.

Search Strategy Trade-off (Speed vs Sense)

  • Manipulate: initial gene allocation; Environment: scarce vs abundant Food/Second.
  • Expectation: Scarce food favors Sense (range); abundant favors Speed (competition to reach).
  • Measure: Which gene increases over generations; time to dominance; population resilience.

Size Advantage Under Scarcity

  • Manipulate: low Food/Second, moderate Food Energy.
  • Expectation: Size (reach/storage) rises; very high Size costs may backfire if metabolism is high.
  • Measure: Mean Size trend, survival through bottlenecks.

Bottlenecks and Founder Effects

  • Protocol: Run, then abruptly reduce Food/Second for 1-2 minutes, restore afterward.
  • Expectation: Gene means drift; post-bottleneck population reflects survivors' traits.
  • Measure: Pre/post means, variance, recovery time.

How To Run Each

  • Set a baseline, change exactly one parameter, run for a fixed time window (e.g., 5-8 minutes at 1x or 2-3 minutes at 4x).
  • Do 2-3 replicates per setting.
  • Record: mean population (last 1-2 minutes), peak population, births/minute, deaths/minute, mean genes.
  • Use Pause + click inspector to sample individuals (energy, age, gene mix).

Controls And Confounders

  • Keep initial gene mix constant across trials (sliders).
  • Use the Speed toggle consistently (1x vs 4x) within a set of trials.
  • Small populations show stronger drift — run repeats or increase initial population for selection-focused labs.

Tips for Faster Results

  • Increase speed to 4x-8x for quicker convergence.
  • Raise Mutation (std) modestly (e.g., 0.05) to explore more variation, then reduce for stabilization.
  • Ensure viability: if crashes occur, raise Food/Second or lower Base Metabolism/Threshold.
